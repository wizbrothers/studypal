    Study Tips

    Expert strategies to help you learn more effectively

    English

    Active Reading

    Highlight key points and take notes as you read. Summarize each paragraph in your own words.

    Vocabulary Building

    Keep a word journal. Write down new words with definitions and example sentences.

    Essay Writing

    Start with an outline. Create a clear thesis statement and support it with evidence from the text.

    Reading Comprehension

    Preview the text before reading. Look at headings, bold words, and questions at the end.

    Science

    Understand, Don't Memorize

    Focus on understanding concepts and processes, not just memorizing facts. Ask "why" and "how".

    Visual Learning

    Draw diagrams and flowcharts. Label parts and explain processes in your own drawings.

    Practice Problems

    Work through practice problems regularly. Science is learned by doing, not just reading.

    Connect to Real Life

    Relate concepts to everyday examples. This makes abstract ideas more concrete and memorable.

    Math

    Practice Daily

    Do a few problems every day. Math skills build on each other, so consistent practice is key.

    Show Your Work

    Write out all steps, even if they seem obvious. This helps you catch mistakes and understand the process.

    Learn from Mistakes

    When you get a problem wrong, figure out exactly where you went wrong and why.

    Master the Basics

    Make sure you understand foundational concepts before moving to advanced topics.

    History

    Create Timelines

    Organize events chronologically. Visual timelines help you understand cause and effect.

    Connect Events

    Look for relationships between events. How did one event lead to another?

    Use Mnemonics

    Create memory tricks for dates and sequences. Songs, acronyms, and stories work great.

    Study with Context

    Understand the why behind events, not just what happened. Consider different perspectives.

    General

    Pomodoro Technique

    Study for 25 minutes, then take a 5-minute break. After 4 sessions, take a longer break.

    Teach Someone Else

    Explaining concepts to others is one of the best ways to deepen your own understanding.

    Minimize Distractions

    Find a quiet study space. Put your phone away or use apps that block social media.

    Take Care of Yourself

    Get enough sleep, eat well, and exercise. Your brain works better when your body is healthy.
